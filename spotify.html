- spotify -

A redesign of Spotify's mobile UX.

I decided to redesign Spotify's mobile UX as an exploration into UX design, as someone who has never approached this sort of project before.

With not much background in UX research and design, I decided to take inspiration from those around me. I first identified user goals and pain points from personal experience with the app, as well as those of my peers.

User Goals

  • listen to and save music in playlists and library
  • discover new music easily
  • share songs with friends - general socializing

Pain Points

  • important feature are hidden
  • inconsistencies in feature presentation across pages
  • overwhelming amount of information presented on each page
  • duplication of information across pages

Information Architecture

Information architecture defines the design structure of a page. Through examination of the way content is organized across the various Spotify pages ('home', 'search', 'your library'), I gleaned two main takeaway - 1) there were many repeated or similarly categorized sections on one page and across multiple pages, and 2) the home page has far too many categories for someone to scroll through.

Design Goals

From my information architecture diagram, as well as my predefined user goals, I decided on the following design goals:

  • reduce the volume of information presented - mobile users are restricted on the amount of space allowed by their devices
  • make functions (e.g. search and filter) consistent and accessible across pages
  • minimize the amount of work done by the user to perform certain functions
